The Problem

The info exists. Nobody can find it.

The Honors handbook is dense. Canvas announcements get buried. Office hours are 9–5. By the time you find what you need, the deadline already passed.

🌙
The deadlines that hit at 11pm

Most of the deadlines I've almost missed were on a Sunday night or a random Tuesday at 11pm. The Honors office isn't open then. Now there's something to ask.

🗂
The handbook is forty pages

Plus Canvas announcements. Plus three department websites that sometimes disagree with each other. The chat reads the documents so you don't have to.

🎓
Stuff your advisor assumes you know

If your parents went to college in the US, you probably already know how things like priority registration or ELA budget approvals are supposed to work. If they didn't, you learn this stuff by getting burned by it.

Research

Find research in your field

Where to look at UTSA and externally, broken down by discipline. Click any card to ask the Oracle for tailored advice.

🧪
STEM · Bio · Chem · CS · Eng · Math · Physics

At UTSA: faculty directory at utsa.edu/research, plus the URA undergraduate-research grants. National: NSF REU sites (apply to 6–10; site acceptance ~5–15%), Amgen Scholars, HHMI EXROP, NIH Summer Internship Program, DAAD RISE (Germany). Most summer deadlines hit in February.

🧬
Health Sciences · Pre-Med · Public Health

At UTSA: NIH-funded MARC and RISE programs for biomedical researchers, plus UT Health San Antonio connections. National: NIH SIP, Mayo Clinic SURF, MD Anderson CPRIT Summer Program, Texas HHS internships, AAMC summer programs.

🧠
Social Sciences · Psychology · Economics

Move 1: cold-email three faculty whose papers you actually read. National: APA STEP, NSF REU in psychology/sociology, NBER (econ), and predoctoral fellowships at Stanford SIEPR, Harvard/Chicago/MIT labs — paid 2-year positions for graduates heading to a PhD.

📜
Humanities · Arts · Languages

At UTSA: URA grants fund humanities and creative work, not only STEM. National: Mellon Mays Undergraduate Fellowship (the high-leverage pre-PhD path for humanities students), Critical Language Scholarship, Beinecke Scholarship (juniors, $34K toward grad school), ACLS dissertation programs in the long run.

⚖️
Business · Policy · Public Service

National: PPIA Junior Summer Institute (free summer policy school for rising seniors), Federal Reserve undergrad analyst programs, congressional and Texas Legislature internships, NBER for empirical econ, McNair Scholars for first-gen and underrepresented students aiming at a PhD.

📩
Cold-Email a UTSA Faculty Mentor

Read one of their recent papers. Reference a specific claim from it in your email. Say what you bring — a class, a tool, a specific question. Ask for a 15-minute conversation, not a lab spot. Send Tue–Thu, 8–10am. Most students never email anyone; sending three this week puts you ahead of your cohort.

Scholarships

The Scholarship Playbook

What review committees actually look for, the deadlines that matter, and the moves that separate winners from also-rans.

🔬
Goldwater Scholarship

For: STEM sophs/juniors targeting a research PhD. Award: $7,500/yr up to 2 yrs. Deadline: campus late Jan. Reviewers want: a 3-page research essay that reads like a junior PI's Specific Aims, plus real lab experience. Tip: start research freshman year — second-semester sophomores rarely have enough runway.

🌍
Fulbright U.S. Student

For: graduating seniors and recent alumni. One year of study, research, or English teaching abroad. Deadline: campus early Sep, national mid-Oct. Reviewers want: defensible country fit, language readiness, project that is feasible in one year, cultural-ambassadorship evidence. Tip: contact host institutions in May — winners know their hosts before submitting.

📚
NSF GRFP

For: seniors entering STEM PhDs and 1st/2nd-year grads. Award: $37K/yr stipend + $16K tuition for 3 yrs. Deadline: mid-Oct. Reviewers want: Intellectual Merit AND Broader Impacts weighted equally. Tip: broader-impacts is where most STEM applicants fumble. List named, completed outreach. "I plan to mentor…" loses to "I taught Saturday Coding Club at Brackenridge."

🏛️
Truman Scholarship

For: juniors committed to public-service careers (gov, nonprofit, advocacy). Award: up to $30K for grad school. Deadline: campus early Jan; UTSA nominates one student. Reviewers want: sustained, named, measurable work on ONE policy issue across years. Tip: Truman doesn't reward generalists. Pick a lane, own it visibly, build a multi-year record.

🌲
Udall Scholarship

For: sophs/juniors on three tracks — environment, Native American health policy, or tribal public policy. Award: up to $7,000. Deadline: early Mar. Reviewers want: deep, demonstrated commitment to one track, not all three. Tip: the environment track is most competitive; the policy tracks have higher acceptance rates with strong applications.

✈️
Gilman · Boren · CLS

Gilman: Pell recipients abroad, up to $5K ($8K for critical-need languages), two cycles per year. Boren: up to $25K for critical-language study abroad with federal-service commitment after. CLS: fully-funded summer language program, deadline mid-Nov. Tip: all three reward applicants who clearly explain why this specific language matters for their career.

🎓
Rhodes · Marshall · Mitchell

For: graduating seniors → UK/Ireland grad study. Award: 2 yrs+ fully funded. Deadlines: October. Reviewers want: institutional-level intellectual leadership — you ran the thing, founded the thing, fixed the thing. Tip: if you can't name 3 distinct leadership accomplishments with measurable impact, this isn't your cycle. Try again next year with a stronger record.

⭐
UTSA Honors-Specific Awards

Internal Honors awards — study-abroad supplements, research grants, named scholarships — are announced through the Monday Message and posted in the Honors portal. Eligibility usually requires Honors standing plus a minimum GPA. Tip: the pool is small. Students who win these read the Monday Message every week. Showing up matters more than at the national level.

🎯
A few things reviewers tend to care about

Specific research questions read better than broad interest areas. "Climate policy" is vague. "How ERCOT models winter-storm load forecasts after Uri" is something a reviewer can imagine you actually working on.

Every essay quietly answers four questions: why this topic, why you specifically, why now, and why this program. If your draft skips one, the reviewer notices.

Broader impacts is not filler. Concrete things you have already done (a workshop you ran, a class you TA'd, students you mentored) beat anything starting with "I plan to."

The single best thing you can do six weeks out is have someone who already won the same scholarship read your essay. Almost nobody does this.

Recommenders need a one-page brief: the prompt, the deadline, what you want them to mention. Send it six weeks out. They will quietly resent two-week notice.

The campus deadline is usually four to eight weeks before the national one, and is where most people lose. Plan backwards from the national date, then keep going.

Playbook

Things I wish I'd done freshman year.

Stuff I see now would have moved the needle most, looking back. Click any card to ask the Oracle for specifics tailored to your major and year.

🔬
Get into a lab this month

Not next semester. This month. Even as a freshman with one CS class on your transcript, faculty take students who show up and bring energy. By the time you're a junior applying for Goldwater, the students who started freshman year have a two-year head start.

🎯
Pick a question, not a field

"I'm interested in AI" gets you nowhere. "I want to study how language models behave when given conflicting instructions" gets you a faculty meeting. Reviewers and mentors respond to specificity. Spend a weekend picking one question worth two years.

🗂️
Build one public artifact

A GitHub with three substantive projects. A personal site that explains what you care about and why. A blog post about something you learned the hard way. Reviewers can't see your grades. They can see your artifacts. Make them findable.

⭐
Start one named thing

Founded, organized, ran — not "member of," not "participated in." A reading group you run. A workshop you teach. A small event you organize. Truman, Rhodes, Marshall, and the broader-impacts half of NSF GRFP all weight this heavily.

📩
Cold-email three faculty

This week. Read one paper from each, reference a specific claim in your email, ask for 15 minutes. Most students never email anyone. Sending three puts you in the top 5% of your cohort by initiative alone. Ask the Oracle for the template.

📚
Read five real papers

Not five textbooks. Five real research papers, published this year, in the area you claim to care about. You learn how researchers actually think, what good questions look like, and how to talk to faculty without sounding like a student.

🗓️
Plan four years backward

Goldwater opens sophomore year. Fulbright happens senior fall. NSF GRFP happens senior fall too. Each one requires 18 months of prep. If you start junior year, you've already lost the cycle. Map your years backward from the deadlines that matter.

📝
Document as you go

A running brag-file. One line per accomplishment, the date, who saw it, what changed because of it. When the Goldwater essay asks for 3 pages on your research record, students who kept this file write it in a weekend. Students who didn't, miss the cycle.
